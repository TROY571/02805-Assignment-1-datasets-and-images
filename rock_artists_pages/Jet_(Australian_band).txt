'''Jet''' are<!-- Don't change "are" to "is"; in Australian English band names are treated as noun plurals. --> an Australian rock band formed in 2001. Founding mainstays are brothers [[Nic Cester|Nic]] (vocals, rhythm guitar, piano) and [[Chris Cester]] (drums, vocals) together with [[Cameron Muncey]] (lead guitar and vocals). They were joined in the following year by [[Mark Wilson (musician)|Mark Wilson]] (bass guitar). The quartet released three studio albums ''[[Get Born]]'' (2003), ''[[Shine On (Jet album)|Shine On]]'' (2006) and ''[[Shaka Rock]]'' (2009) before disbanding in 2012. ''Get Born'' is their highest charting work, which peaked at number&nbsp;one in [[ARIA Charts|Australia]], top&nbsp;20 in the United Kingdom and top&nbsp;30 in the United States. Its lead single "[[Are You Gonna Be My Girl]]" (2003) reached the top&nbsp;20 in Australia and UK and top&nbsp;30 in the US. Both ''Shine On'' and ''Shaka Rock'' are Australian top&nbsp;five albums, while their other top&nbsp;20 singles are "[[Look What You've Done]]" (2004), "[[Put Your Money Where Your Mouth Is (song)|Put Your Money Where Your Mouth Is]]" (2006) and "[[She's a Genius]]" (2009).

At the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2004]] they headed the leader board with six&nbsp;wins: [[ARIA Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]], [[ARIA Award for Best Group|Best Group]], [[ARIA Award for Best Rock Album|Best Rock Album]] and [[ARIA Award for Breakthrough Artist – Album|Breakthrough Artist – Album]] for ''Get Born'' as well as [[ARIA Award for Single of the Year|Single of the Year]] and [[ARIA Award for Breakthrough Artist – Single|Breakthrough Artist – Single]] for "Are You Gonna Be My Girl". At the [[APRA Awards (Australia)|APRA Awards]] that single won Most Performed Australian Work Overseas in [[APRA Music Awards of 2005|2005]], [[APRA Music Awards of 2006|2006]] and [[APRA Music Awards of 2007|2007]]. Jet reformed in 2016 but released no new studio albums and separated again in 2019. They reunited for a second time in 2023 and were inducted into the [[ARIA Hall of Fame]] in November [[2023 ARIA Music Awards|that year]].

==History==
===Formation and ''Dirty Sweet'' (2001–2003)===
[[File:NicCester Chicago04.jpg|thumb|Founding mainstay, [[Nic Cester]] on lead vocals and guitar, 2004]]
The Cester brothers, [[Nic Cester|Nic]] and [[Chris Cester|Chris]], grew up in [[Dingley Village]] and attended [[St Bede's College (Mentone)|St Bede's College, Mentone]], Melbourne.<ref name="McFarlane">{{cite book |last1=McFarlane |first1=Ian |author-link1=Ian McFarlane |last2=Jenkins |first2=Jeff (Foreword) |title=[[Encyclopedia of Australian Rock and Pop|The Encyclopedia of Australian Rock and Pop]] |chapter=Encyclopedia entry for 'Jet' |date=2017 |location=[[Gisborne, Victoria|Gisborne, VIC]] |publisher=Third Stone Press |edition=2nd |pages=245–246 |isbn=978-0-9953856-0-3 }}</ref> They listened to their father John's [[classic rock]] records from the 1960s and 1970s.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6hOdHbs95go | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211118/6hOdHbs95go| archive-date=2021-11-18 | url-status=live|title=Jet, the early years |publisher=YouTube |date=2009-07-01 |access-date=2012-04-13}}{{cbignore}}</ref> While still at school Nic formed a group by 1995, which [[Cameron Muncey]] joined.<ref name="Nimmervoll">{{cite web |url=http://www.howlspace.com.au/en5/jet/jet.htm |title=Jet |first1=Ed |last1=Nimmervoll |author-link1=Ed Nimmervoll |website=HowlSpace |via=National Library of Australia |archive-url=http://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20070829091427/http://www.howlspace.com.au/en5/jet/jet.htm |archive-date=29 August 2007 |access-date=5 October 2023 |url-status=live }}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> Jet's influences include the Beatles, the Rolling Stones, the Faces, AC/DC and [[T. Rex (band)|T. Rex]].<ref name="ODonnell">{{Cite book |title=[[100 Best Australian Albums]] |last1=O'Donnell |first1=John |author-link1=John O'Donnell (music journalist) |last2=Creswell |first2=Toby |author-link2=Toby Creswell |last3=Mathieson |first3=Craig |author-link3=Craig Mathieson |publisher=Hardie Grant Books |date=October 2010 |location=[[Prahran, Victoria|Prahran, Vic]] |isbn=978-1-74066-955-9 }}</ref>{{rp|230–231}} According to Nic, Australian [[power pop]] band [[You Am I]] had the biggest influence on their developing musical tastes:{{blockquote|"''[[Hi Fi Way]]'' was the most important album of my generation... I think everyone our age, who played guitar, played You Am I songs for the first time in front of their school assembly... That was the record that made you realise you could be in an Australian band, you didn't have to be a grunge band and you didn't have to be influenced by American bands. It changed everything."<ref name="ODonnell" />{{rp|230–231}}<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.dailytelegraph.com.au/lifestyle/sunday-magazine/wizards-of-oz/story-e6frf039-1111112857646 |title=Wizards of Oz |author=Tiffany Baker |date=21 January 2007 |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |access-date=16 November 2010}}</ref>}}

Nic, on lead vocals and guitar, decided to form a [[garage rock]] band, in 2001, with Muncey on lead guitar and vocals, before Chris joined on drums and vocals.<ref name="McFarlane" /><ref name="Scatena">{{cite news |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231005030032/https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/happy-landings-all-over-the-globe-as-jet-finally-flames-out-20120330-1w37v.html |title=Happy Landings all Over the Globe as Jet Finally Flames Out |first=Dino |last=Scatena |newspaper=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]] |date=31 March 2012 |access-date=5 October 2023 |url=https://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/happy-landings-all-over-the-globe-as-jet-finally-flames-out-20120330-1w37v.html |url-status=live |url-access=subscription |archive-date=5 October 2023 }}</ref> Bass guitarist Doug Armstrong, who completed the line-up,<ref name="McFarlane" /> had met the Cesters while working at their father's spice factory. The quartet trialled various band names, Mojo Filter, Hi-Fidelity and Duosonic, before settling for Jet in 2002 – from the song of the [[Jet (song)|same name]] (originally by Paul McCartney and Wings).<ref name="McFarlane" /><ref name="Nimmervoll" /> Their early repertoire was cover versions of You Am I songs and "whatever else was going 'round."<ref name="Nimmervoll" /> "Radio Song" from their debut album ''[[Get Born]]'' was written about difficulties they had obtaining recognition at that time.<ref name="Undercover 1">{{cite web |url=http://www.undercover.com.au/reviews/urjetgetborn.html |title=Jet, ''Get Born'' |website=Undercover |via=[[National Library of Australia]] |archive-url=http://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20050601043144/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/11299/20050518-0000/www.undercover.com.au/reviews/urjetgetborn.html |archive-date=1 June 2005 |access-date=4 October 2023 |url-status=bot: unknown }}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref>

Jet supported Melbourne punk rock band the Specimens as an opening act at The Duke of Windsor hotel, [[Windsor, Victoria|Windsor]].<ref name="Levin" /><ref name="Aus Story" /> [[Talent manager]] and booking agent Dave Powell was filling-in for the venue's sound engineer,<ref name="Nimmervoll" /> "I realised the singer could actually sing. I was blown away. They had a lot of potential and I didn't have a lot of bands coming through who were like that."<ref name="Levin">{{cite web |url=http://fasterlouder.junkee.com/jet-rise-fall-melbournes-nu-rock-scene/866437/amp.html |title=Jet, and the Rise and Fall of Melbourne's Nu-rock Scene |first1=Darren |last1=Levin |website=[[Junkee Media|FasterLouder]] |date=13 September 2016 |via=National Library of Australia |archive-url=http://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20170227195645/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/151586/20170228-0632/fasterlouder.junkee.com/jet-rise-fall-melbournes-nu-rock-scene/866437/amp.html |archive-date=27 February 2017 |access-date=4 October 2023 |url-status=live }}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> Powell signed the then-unnamed group to his management firm.<ref name="Nimmervoll" /><ref name="Aus Story">{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/austory/content/2011/s3199996.htm |title=''Australian Story'' - 25/04/2011: 'In the Jet Stream' - Transcript |website=[[Australian Story]] |date=25 April 2011 |via=[[National Library of Australia]] |archive-url=http://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20151128133943/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/31958/20151129-0000/www.abc.net.au/austory/content/2011/s3199996.htm |archive-date=28 November 2015 |access-date=5 October 2023 |url-status=dead }}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> Nic recalled, "Duke of Windsor became our headquarters. [Powell] would be taking calls from you know, executives in Los Angeles from the keg room you know, that was his office."<ref name="Aus Story" /> The members had met [[Mark Wilson (musician)|Mark Wilson]] in 2002 during his performance at that same venue and asked him to replace Armstrong on bass guitar.<ref name="McFarlane" /><ref name="Nimmervoll" /><ref name="Ca$inos Bio" /> Wilson had been in a [[Geelong]]-based band, the Ca$inos.<ref name="Ca$inos Bio">{{cite web |url=http://www.musicaustralia.org/apps/MA?function=showDetail&currentMapsRecord=NAMO~275&itemSeq=10&total=44&returnFunction=searchResults&simpleTerm=Computer+music. |title=Ca$inos |website=[[Music Australia (online resource)|Music Australia]] |date=14 November 2004 |via=National Library of Australia |archive-url=http://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20050718173247/http://www.musicaustralia.org/apps/MA?function=showDetail&currentMapsRecord=NAMO~275&itemSeq=10&total=44&returnFunction=searchResults&simpleTerm=Computer+music. |archive-date=18 July 2005 |access-date=5 October 2023 |url-status=live }}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref>

Late in 2002 Jet independently issued a four-track debut extended play ''[[Dirty Sweet]]'', with distribution by Rubber Records.<ref name="McFarlane" /><ref name="Nimmervoll" /><ref name="Levin" /> Its initial pressing of 1000&nbsp;copies was sold out.<ref>{{cite web|url={{AllMusic|class=artist|id=p559321|pure_url=yes}}|publisher=[[Allmusic]]|title=Jet|access-date=28 April 2009|first=MacKenzie|last=Wilson}}</ref> An ''[[NME]]'' writer had obtained a copy of the group's single "Take It or Leave It" from ''Dirty Sweet'' and described it as a mixture of the Rolling Stones and AC/DC.<ref name="Levin" /> [[Elektra Records]] executives signed the band for a recording contract and re-released ''Dirty Sweet'' in May 2003.<ref name="McFarlane" /><ref name="Wilson2">{{cite web | url = {{AllMusic|class=album|id=r641739|pure_url=yes}} |title=''Dirty Sweet'' &#91;EP&#93; Jet |last1=Wilson |first1=MacKenzie |publisher=[[AllMusic]] |access-date=12 July 2013 }}</ref>

===''Get Born'' (2003–2005)===
{{Main|Get Born}}
[[File:Chris Cester.jpg|thumb|Founding mainstay drummer [[Chris Cester]], onstage during [[Sound Relief]], 2009]]
Jet entered the [[Sunset Sound Studios]], Los Angeles with [[Dave Sardy]] producing their debut album ''Get Born'' (September 2003).<ref name="McFarlane" /><ref name="Levin" /> Sardy had produced work for [[Marilyn Manson (band)|Marilyn Manson]] and [[the Dandy Warhols]].<ref name="Sardy Creds">{{cite web |url=https://www.allmusic.com/artist/dave-sardy-mn0000787049/credits |title=Dave Sardy {{!}} Credits |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=4 October 2023 }}</ref> The Australian quartet used [[Billy Preston]] on keyboards for two&nbsp;tracks.<ref name="Sendra">{{cite web |url=https://www.allmusic.com/album/get-born-mw0000324759 |title=Jet – ''Get Born'' Album Reviews, Songs & More |first=Tim |last=Sendra |publisher=AllMusic |access-date=4 October 2023 }}</ref> ''Get Born''{{'}}s title references a lyric from Bob Dylan's "[[Subterranean Homesick Blues]]" (1965) "Ah get born, keep warm."<ref name="Nimmervoll" /> It reached number&nbsp;one in [[ARIA Charts|Australia]] and was accredited 8× platinum by [[Australian Recording Industry Association]] (ARIA).<ref name="AUS Charts">{{cite web |url=https://australian-charts.com/showinterpret.asp?interpret=Jet |title=Discography Jet |first=Steffen |last=Hung |publisher=Australian Charts Portal (Hung Medien) |access-date=4 October 2023 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite certification |region=Australia |type=album |certyear=2004 }}</ref> It peaked in the top&nbsp;20 in the UK and top&nbsp;30 in the US,<ref name="UK">{{cite web|url=http://www.zobbel.de/cluk/CLUK_J.HTM |title=Chart Log UK: Candy J. - JX |website=Zobbel.de |access-date=2011-09-14 }}</ref><ref>{{cite magazine|url={{BillboardURLbyName|artist=jet|chart=Billboard 200}}|title=Jet Album & Song Chart History: Billboard 200|magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]|access-date=2010-06-13}}</ref> and was certified platinum in both countries by [[British Phonographic Industry]] (BPI) and [[Recording Industry Association of America]] (RIAA), respectively.<ref name="BPI">{{cite web|url=http://www.bpi.co.uk/certified-awards.aspx |title=Certified Awards Search |publisher=[[British Phonographic Industry]] |access-date=16 August 2013 |format=To access, enter the keyword "Jet" and choose the "Search by: Artist" option |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130124005813/http://www.bpi.co.uk/certified-awards.aspx |archive-date= 24 January 2013 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.riaa.com/goldandplatinumdata.php?table=SEARCH_RESULTS&artist=Jet&title=GetBorn&format=ALBUM&go=Search&perPage=50|title=RIAA - Gold & Platinum - 13&nbsp;June 2010: Jet certified albums|publisher=Recording Industry Association of America|access-date=2010-06-13}}</ref>

The Rolling Stones offered Jet a support slot on the Australian leg of their 2003 tour.<ref name="McFarlane" /><ref name="Wilson2" /> Including their own headlining tours, the Australian group played more than 200&nbsp;shows during that 2003.<ref name="high">{{cite magazine |magazine=[[Rolling Stone]] |date=7 April 2004 |title=Jet's High Times |url=https://www.rollingstone.com/artists/jet/articles/story/5939242/jets_high_times |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061022200307/http://www.rollingstone.com/artists/jet/articles/story/5939242/jets_high_times |url-status=dead |archive-date=22 October 2006 |first=Jenny |last=Eliscu |access-date=4 October 2023 }}</ref> ''Get Born''{{'}}s singles "[[Are You Gonna Be My Girl]]" and "[[Rollover DJ]]" were listed at number&nbsp;one and number&nbsp;nineteen, respectively, on the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2003]]. "Are You Gonna Be My Girl" appeared on the soundtracks for the video games ''[[Madden NFL 2004]]'', ''[[Guitar Hero: On Tour]]'', ''[[Rock Band (video game)|Rock Band]]'', and ''[[Strictly Come Dancing]]''; the 2006 animated film ''[[Flushed Away]]''; the live-action films ''[[Eurotrip]]'' and ''[[What Happens in Vegas]]''; and as part of two major worldwide advertising campaigns for [[Vodafone]] and [[Apple Inc.|Apple's]] iPod and [[iMac]].

"[[Rollover DJ]]" is used on the soundtrack of [[PlayStation 2]] game ''[[Gran Turismo 4]]''. ''Get Born'' also includes the song "Timothy", dedicated to Muncey's deceased brother – due to its sensitive nature the quartet rarely play the track live.<ref>{{cite magazine |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20040602192926/http://inmusicwetrust.com/articles/65h04.html |url=http://www.inmusicwetrust.com/articles/65h04.html |title=Interview: Jet: Australian Rock Imports |magazine=In Music We Trust |date=April 2004 |issue=65 |access-date=4 October 2023 |archive-date=2 June 2004 |url-status=live }}</ref> In early 2004, Jet teamed up with [[The Vines (band)|the Vines]] and [[the Living End]] on The Aussie Invasion tour of US. A new Jet song, "Hold On", not included on ''Get Born'', was used on the [[Spider-Man 2 (soundtrack)|''Spider-Man 2'' soundtrack]] that year. TV and film producer [[J. J. Abrams]] contacted them to record a song for the Season 4 of ''[[Alias (TV series)|Alias]]''. "[[Cold Hard Bitch]]" from ''Get Born'' was used, and used during a frenetic scene of two [[Central Intelligence Agency|CIA]] agents sprinting down a [[Hong Kong]] street, giving the band further US exposure.

Later in 2004 Jet received nominations for the annual US [[Radio Music Awards]], including Artist of the Year: Rock Radio, Artist of the Year: Rock Alternative Radio, and Song of the Year: Rock Radio (for "Cold Hard Bitch"). They also received the [[American Music Awards of 2004|32nd Annual American Music Awards]] nomination as Favorite Artist – Alternative Music, as well as three nominations at the [[2004 MTV Video Music Awards]], held in Miami in August. "Are You Gonna Be My Girl" was nominated for [[MTV Video Music Award for Best Rock Video|Best Rock Video]], [[MTV Video Music Award for Best New Artist|Best New Artist]] and [[MTV Video Music Award for Best Editing|Best Editing]] in a video. Jet performed "Are You Gonna Be My Girl" live at the ceremony and picked up the award for Best Rock Video,<ref name="MTV Won">{{cite news |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210609145443/http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/jet-win-mtv-award-20040830-gdjnby.html |title=Jet Win MTV Award |newspaper=The Sydney Morning Herald |date=30 August 2004 |access-date=5 October 2023 |url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/music/jet-win-mtv-award-20040830-gdjnby.html |url-access=subscription |archive-date=9 June 2021 }}</ref> dedicating it to the Cesters' late father.

Following their success in the US, back home the group headed the leader board at the [[ARIA Music Awards of 2004]] by winning six categories: [[ARIA Award for Album of the Year|Album of the Year]], [[ARIA Award for Best Group|Best Group]], [[ARIA Award for Best Rock Album|Best Rock Album]] and [[ARIA Award for Breakthrough Artist – Album|Breakthrough Artist – Album]] for ''Get Born'' as well as [[ARIA Award for Single of the Year|Single of the Year]] and [[ARIA Award for Breakthrough Artist – Single|Breakthrough Artist – Single]] for "Are You Gonna Be My Girl". They were also nominated for [[ARIA Award for Highest-Selling Album|Highest-Selling Album]]. At the ceremony in October the quartet performed "Are You Gonna Be My Girl";<ref name="Donovan" /> while Nic provided lead vocals on a cover version of Stevie Wright's "[[Evie (song)#The Wrights version|Evie (part 1)]]" as a member of an Australian super-group [[The Wrights (Australian band)|the Wrights]], with Spiderbait's [[Kram (musician)|Kram]] (drums), the Living End's [[Chris Cheney]] (guitar), [[Dallas Crane]]'s Pat Bourke (bass guitar) and You Am I's [[David Lane (musician)|Davey Lane]] (guitar).<ref name="Cashmere">{{cite web |url=http://www.undercover.com.au/news/2005/jan05/20050107_wrights.html |title=Nic Cester Supergroup Christened the Wrights |first1=Paul |last1=Cashmere |author-link1=Paul Cashemere |website=Undercover |date=7 January 2005 |via=National Library of Australia |archive-url=http://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20051118034940/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/11299/20051118-0000/www.undercover.com.au/news/2005/jan05/20050107_wrights.html |archive-date=18 November 2005 |access-date=5 October 2023 |url-status=bot: unknown }}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> ''Get Born''{{'}}s "[[Look What You've Done]]" (No.&nbsp;24) and "Get What You Need" (No.&nbsp;89) were listed on the [[Triple J Hottest 100, 2004]].<ref name="Triple J 2004">{{cite web |url=https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/hottest100/archive/search/?year=2004 |title=Search {{!}} Hottest 100 Archive: 2004 |date=11 November 2017 |publisher=Triple J (Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC)) |access-date=7 October 2023 }}</ref>

During a show at the [[MCG]] in October 2005, Nic declared that he considered his group to be "up there with the greatests, with fucking [[John Lennon|Lennon]] and the Stones." This caused controversy but was settled when Australian music magazines began publishing articles comparing Jet's albums with those of famous 1960s and 1970s bands.<ref name="Schuler">{{cite magazine |last=Schuler |first=Rachel |date=3 November 2014 |title=Where Are They Now: Jet |url=http://www.infectiousmagazine.com/where-are-they-now-jet/ |magazine=Infectious Magazine |location=Australia |access-date=6 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150327102521/http://www.infectiousmagazine.com/where-are-they-now-jet/ |archive-date=27 March 2015 }}</ref> ''Get Born'' is listed at No.&nbsp;89 in the book, ''[[100 Best Australian Albums]]'' (October 2010).<ref name="ODonnell" />{{rp|230}} The authors described it as "free of the neurotic self-examination that passes for meaningful songwriting in the post-Nirvana era."<ref name="ODonnell" />{{rp|230}}

===''Shine On'' (2006–2008)===
[[File:Mark Wilson (musician) jet 2004.jpg|thumb|[[Mark Wilson (musician)|Mark Wilson]] on bass guitar (centre), March 2004]]
"[[Put Your Money Where Your Mouth Is (Jet Song)|Put Your Money Where Your Mouth Is]]" was released to radio as a single in August 2006 ahead of their second album, ''[[Shine On (Jet album)|Shine On]]''. It followed on 30 September (2 October in the UK, 3 October in the US) with a physical listing of 15&nbsp;tracks. An extra track, "Coming Home Soon", was available only from the ITunes Store. ''Shine On'' debuted at No.&nbsp;3 on the Australian charts and was accredited platinum by ARIA in 2006.<ref name="AUS Charts" /><ref>{{Cite certification |region=Australia |type=album |certyear=2008 }}</ref> Critical reaction to the album was mixed,<ref name="McFarlane" /> with British music magazine ''[[NME]]''{{'}}s Paul McNamee calling it "another joyfully old-fashioned rock'n'roll album immersed in the classics."<ref>{{cite magazine |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/jet/8037 |title=Reviews – Jet: ''Shine On'' |first1=Paul |last1=McNamee |magazine=[[NME]] |date=29 September 2006 |access-date=5 October 2023 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20061124082900/https://www.nme.com/reviews/jet/8037 |archive-date=24 November 2006 }}</ref> US music blogger Ray Suzuki of ''[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]]'' gave the album 0.0 out of 10 and posted a video of a chimpanzee urinating in its own mouth instead of a written review.<ref name="McFarlane" /><ref>{{cite web |url=https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/9464-shine-on/ |title=Jet: ''Shine On'' |first1=Ray |last1=Suzuki |website=[[Pitchfork Media|Pitchfork]] |date=2 October 2006 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20231001115352/https://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/9464-shine-on/ |archive-date=1 October 2023 }}</ref> "Put Your Money Where Your Mouth Is" appeared on the "[[Chuck (TV series)|Chuck]]" TV series pilot episode, "[[Chuck Versus the Intersect]]". ===''Shaka Rock'' and disbandment (2009–2012)===
[[File:Jet as the opening act of an Oasis Concert. - 42838413 05.jpg|thumb|Jet performing live (2004)]]
In a video on their website band members talked about writing and recording songs at Atlantic Sound Studios in New York City; Chris quipped: "We've got to stop writing; we got too many songs." Jet returned to Australia to play a show in Melbourne on 17 December to debut new material, "Goodbye Hollywood", "Walk", "Start the Show", "Black Hearts", "She's a Genius", "Seventeen", and "Beat On Repeat". Muncey expressed that he "really, really loves the song 'Seventeen'... I think is sort of a step forward for us songwriting-wise. It's sorta got everything wrapped into one, a storyline, and sad to say melancholy. It's rock and roll, it's rocking, it's got all these other albums to it, you know, so I find it really interesting."<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mtvasia.com/News/200908/20018356.html |title=Jet: Shaking Up the Airwaves with ''Shaka Rock'' |first1=Daryl |last1=Tan |date=20 August 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090827123732/http://www.mtvasia.com/News/200908/20018356.html |archive-date=27 August 2009 }}</ref>

==Activism==
In 2007 Jet members joined a public campaign by [[People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals]] (PETA), which called for an end to [[Seal hunting#Canada|Canada's annual seal hunt]].<ref name="Eliezer">{{cite web |url=http://www.themusic.com.au/imm_display7f3d.html |title=Christie Eliezer's Music Business News |first1=Christie |last1=Eliezer |website=In Music & Media |date=22 May 2007 |via=National Library of Australia |archive-url=http://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20090218024857/http://pandora.nla.gov.au/pan/10761/20090218-0018/www.themusic.com.au/imm_display7f3d.html |archive-date=18 February 2009 |access-date=6 October 2023 |url-status=bot: unknown }}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref> The petition was signed by some 150,000 names, which included fellow Australian musicians [[John Butler (musician)|John Butler]], members of [[Something for Kate]], [[Red Riders]] and the Vines.<ref name="Eliezer" />

===The Fred Hollows Foundation===
In May 2008, Jet released a video clip on [[YouTube]], which is tribute to the work of the late [[Fred Hollows]] and The Fred Hollows Foundation.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://hollows.org |title=The Fred Hollows Foundation|website=Hollows.org}}</ref> Nic highlighted Hollow's work to restore sight to people blinded by [[cataract]]s in developing countries, which was ongoing 15&nbsp;years after his death. "Hollows was such a big figure in Australia and he had a huge impact, but he passed away when there was still work to do," Cester said in a statement. "This clip is a bit of a reminder, hopefully it encourages people to make a donation to keep [Hollow]'s work going."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.ninemsn.com.au/article.aspx?id=569559 |title=Jet Releasing Fred Hollows Tribute Clip (Nine-MSN) |publisher=News.ninemsn.com.au |access-date=2011-09-14 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090112091123/http://news.ninemsn.com.au/article.aspx?id=569559 |archive-date=2009-01-12}}</ref> The video tribute uses Jet's song "Shine On" along with images compiled by [[The Fred Hollows Foundation]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://optuszoo.news.ninemsn.com.au/article.aspx?id=569559 |title=Jet releasing Fred Hollows tribute clip (Nine-MSN) |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090112083805/http://optuszoo.news.ninemsn.com.au/article.aspx?id=569559 |archive-date=2009-01-12}}</ref>
